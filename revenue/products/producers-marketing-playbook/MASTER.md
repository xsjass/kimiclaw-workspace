# Chapter 2: Pricing — How to Charge What You're Worth

## The Pricing Psychology
Artists don't buy beats. They buy the feeling of finding the perfect beat.
Your price signals your quality before they even press play.

## The Tiered Pricing Model

### Tier 1: Basic Lease — $15-30
- MP3 file only
- 1,000-2,500 unit sales limit
- Non-exclusive (beat stays for sale)
- No monetization rights (streaming only)
- Perfect for: Budget artists, testing beats

### Tier 2: Premium Lease — $50-100
- WAV + MP3 + stems
- 5,000-10,000 unit sales limit
- Non-exclusive
- Monetization rights (YouTube, Spotify)
- Perfect for: Serious artists building catalogs

### Tier 3: Exclusive — $200-1,000+
- WAV + MP3 + stems + trackout
- Unlimited sales
- Exclusive (beat removed from store)
- Full monetization rights
- Perfect for: Artists with budgets, labels

## Platform Fee Comparison (What You Actually Keep)

| Platform | Your Cut | On $50 Sale | On $500 Sale |
|----------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| BeatStars Free | 70% | $35 | $350 |
| BeatStars PRO | 100% | $50 | $500 |
| Airbit Free | 80% | $40 | $400 |
| Airbit Platinum | 100% | $50 | $500 |
| Traktrain | 85% | $42.50 | $425 |
| **BeatsHeaven Free** | **91%** | **$45.50** | **$455** |
| **BeatsHeaven PRO** | **100%** | **$50** | **$500** |

## The Pricing Anchor Technique
Always show your highest price first.
"Exclusive: $500 | Premium: $75 | Lease: $25"
This makes $75 feel like a bargain.

## Dynamic Pricing Strategy
- New producer: Start at $15/lease, build reputation
- Established: $30-50/lease, $100-200/exclusive
- Known name: $50-100/lease, $500-2,000/exclusive

## The Discount Trap
Never discount your main prices. Instead:
- Bundle deals (5 beats for price of 4)
- First-time buyer discount (10% off first order)
- Email subscriber exclusive (hidden beat packs)

## Key Takeaway
Price based on value delivered, not hours worked. Your beat took 3 hours to make. The artist's career might take off because of it.
# Chapter 3: Platform Selection — Where to Sell Your Beats

## The Platform Landscape

### BeatStars
**Best for:** High traffic, established marketplace
**Pros:**
- Massive buyer traffic
- Built-in player + store
- Co-producer splits
- YouTube Content ID
**Cons:**
- 30% commission on free plan
- Saturated market
- Hard to stand out
**Pricing:** Free (30% fee) / $19.99/mo (100% keep)

### Airbit
**Best for:** Clean interface, good analytics
**Pros:**
- Modern design
- Detailed analytics
- Instant delivery
- Free plan available
**Cons:**
- Less traffic than BeatStars
- 20% commission on free plan
**Pricing:** Free (20% fee) / $19.99/mo (100% keep)

### Traktrain
**Best for:** Curated, exclusive feel
**Pros:**
- Application-only (quality signal)
- 15% commission
- Clean, minimal interface
- Good for exclusives
**Cons:**
- Must apply to join
- Smaller buyer pool
**Pricing:** 15% commission (no monthly fee)

### BeatsHeaven
**Best for:** Lowest fees, mobile-first, global reach
**Pros:**
- 9% commission (lowest in industry)
- 0% commission on PRO plan
- Mobile-first design
- Built for India + global markets
- Instant license delivery
- Zero buyer fees
**Cons:**
- Newer platform (growing traffic)
- Smaller than BeatStars (for now)
**Pricing:** Free (9% fee) / $14.99/mo PRO (0% fee)

## The Multi-Platform Strategy
Don't put all eggs in one basket.

**Recommended Setup:**
1. **Primary:** BeatsHeaven (lowest fees, best margins)
2. **Secondary:** BeatStars (traffic volume)
3. **Tertiary:** Airbit (backup + diversity)

**Sync Strategy:**
- Same beat names across platforms
- Same pricing tiers
- Same descriptions (with platform-specific CTAs)
- Different exclusive prices (higher on curated platforms)

## Platform Migration Guide
Moving from another platform? Here's how:
1. Export all beat metadata (CSV if available)
2. Re-upload to new platform with optimized descriptions
3. Update social media links
4. Email existing customers about new store
5. Run "grand opening" promotion

## Key Takeaway
The platform with the lowest fees wins long-term. A 10% difference in fees compounds to thousands of dollars per year.

# Chapter 7: Beat Licensing — Protect Your Work, Maximize Revenue

## Why Licensing Matters
- 60% of producers have never sold an exclusive
- 40% don't understand the difference between lease and exclusive
- **Result:** Lost revenue, legal disputes, burned bridges

## The Three Main Licenses

### 1. Basic Lease (Non-Exclusive)
**What the buyer gets:**
- MP3 file
- 1,000-2,500 unit sales limit
- Non-exclusive (beat stays in store)
- No monetization rights
- Cannot use in music videos

**What you keep:**
- All ownership
- Can sell same beat to unlimited artists
- Can use in your own projects

**Price:** $15-30

### 2. Premium Lease (Non-Exclusive)
**What the buyer gets:**
- WAV + MP3 + individual stems (trackouts)
- 5,000-10,000 unit sales limit
- Non-exclusive
- Monetization rights (YouTube, Spotify)
- Can use in music videos

**What you keep:**
- All ownership
- Can sell same beat to others (up to limit)
- Can use in your own projects

**Price:** $50-150

### 3. Exclusive License
**What the buyer gets:**
- WAV + MP3 + stems + trackouts
- Unlimited sales
- Full monetization rights
- Can use anywhere (videos, movies, games)
- Beat removed from your store

**What you keep:**
- Producer credit (must be credited)
- Cannot resell to anyone else
- Can use in your portfolio (with permission)

**Price:** $200-2,000+

## Contract Essentials

### Must-Include Clauses
1. **License Type** — Clearly state which tier
2. **Usage Rights** — What they can/can't do
3. **Sales Limits** — Exact number (if applicable)
4. **Term** — Perpetual or time-limited
5. **Credit Requirements** — How you must be credited
6. **Modification Rights** — Can they edit the beat?
7. **Sample Clearance** — Who's responsible?
8. **Refund Policy** — No refunds on digital goods

### Sample Clearance Warning
If your beat contains samples (songs, vocals, movie clips):
- YOU are responsible for clearing the sample
- The artist is NOT responsible
- Uncleared samples = lawsuits
- **Solution:** Use royalty-free samples only

## The Upsell Strategy

### Automatic Upsells
- After lease purchase: "Upgrade to Premium for $40 more"
- After premium purchase: "Make it exclusive for $200 more"
- Before checkout: "Bundle deal: 5 beats for price of 4"

### Email Upsells
- Day 7 after lease: "Your lease is selling well. Upgrade?"
- Day 30: "Artist hit their limit. Time to go exclusive?"

## Platform-Specific Notes

### BeatStars
- Auto-generates licenses based on your settings
- Content ID available for YouTube
- Co-producer splits supported

### Airbit
- Custom license templates
- Instant delivery
- PDF license generation

### BeatsHeaven
- Auto-generated licenses (all 3 tiers)
- Instant delivery
- Built-in sample clearance warnings
- PRO plan: Custom contract branding

## Key Takeaway
Licensing isn't bureaucracy. It's how you scale from $500/month to $5,000/month. One well-structured exclusive at $500 = 20 leases at $25. Work smarter.
# Chapter 8: Scaling — From Side Hustle to Full-Time

## The Scaling Mindset
**Stage 1:** Making beats, hoping someone buys (hobby)
**Stage 2:** Consistent sales, understanding marketing (side hustle)
**Stage 3:** Predictable revenue, systems in place (business)
**Stage 4:** Team, automation, multiple streams (empire)

## When to Quit Your Day Job
**The 6-Month Rule:**
- 6 months of consistent $3K+/month from beats
- 6-month emergency fund saved
- Health insurance figured out
- Tax planning in place
- **Then** consider going full-time.

## Automation Systems

### Content Automation
- Batch produce 30 beats in one weekend
- Schedule 30 days of social posts using Later/Buffer
- Use email automation (welcome sequence, abandoned cart)
- Repurpose content (YouTube → TikTok clips → Instagram Reels)

### Administrative Automation
- Auto-responders for common questions
- Instant license delivery (all platforms do this)
- Automated invoicing (Stripe + QuickBooks)
- Calendar scheduling (Calendly for consultations)

### What to Automate First
1. Email sequences (highest ROI)
2. Social media scheduling (saves hours/week)
3. License delivery (customers expect instant)
4. Analytics reporting (track what works)

## Hiring Your First Team Member

- Upload beats to platforms

- Reply to basic emails
